Your Weight Across the Solar System

How It Works

Your weight is actually the force of gravity pulling on your mass. Different planets have different masses and sizes, which means they have different gravitational pulls.

The formula used to calculate your weight on different planets is:

Weight on Planet = Weight on Earth x (Planet's Gravity / Earth's Gravity)

For example, if you weigh 70kg on Earth, you would weigh only about 26.6kg on Mars because Mars has approximately 38% of Earth's gravity.
